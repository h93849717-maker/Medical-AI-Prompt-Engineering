# Medical-AI-Prompt-Engineering
Optimizing Gemini 1.5 Flash for empathetic patient communication and surgical risk reframing
🏥 Medical-AI-Prompt-Engineering
Optimizing Gemini 1.5 Flash for empathetic patient communication and surgical risk reframing
📝 Project Overview
This project demonstrates how to steer Google Gemini to communicate complex medical data with empathy. Moving from a NEET (Medical) background, I identified that AI often presents surgical risks in a cold, "robotic" way that spikes patient cortisol levels.

🛠️ The Intervention
Reframing Logic: Engineered the model to shift focus from "4% Risk" to "96% Success Rate."

Clinical Authority: Programmed System Instructions to emphasize the 10+ years of surgical expertise and "capable hands."

Actionable Recovery: Focused the response on a structured "1-month rest period" to give patients a sense of control.

⚙️ Technical Stack
Model: Gemini 3 Flash Preview (via Google AI Studio)

Parameters: Temperature 0.5 (for balanced accuracy/warmth)

Method: System Instruction Persona Mapping

Thinking Level: HIGH

🎯 Problem & Solution
The Problem: Identified a "clinical coldness" in standard LLM responses where AI presented surgical risks (e.g., "4% complication rate") in a way that increased patient anxiety.

The Solution: Engineered a custom persona using Gemini 1.5 Flash to reframe technical data into empathetic, trust-building communication.

🚀 Key Technical Implementations:
Logic Reframing: Programmed the model to systematically pivot from "risk-focus" to "success-focus" (e.g., reframing 4% risk as a 96% success rate).

System Instructions: Authored complex system prompts to mandate the inclusion of clinical authority (highlighting the surgeon’s 10+ years of study).

Parameter Tuning: Optimized model Temperature (0.5) to balance factual medical accuracy with human-centric warmth.

Outcome: Successfully transformed robotic "error-style" outputs into patient-centered recovery plans, focusing on a structured "1-month rest period."
